# Regulatory Case Management System – Retrospective Business Analysis

## Overview:

This repository presents a retrospective business analysis and solution reconstruction for an enterprise regulatory case management system implemented at a state-level regulatory agency in the United States. The agency comprises more than ten divisions spanning consumer protection, utilities oversight, and other regulatory functions, each with distinct workflows and compliance requirements.

Completed as a collaborative effort within a business analyst training cohort, the project demonstrates end-to-end BA practices—from requirements elicitation through process design and documentation.

## My Contributions

- Requirements Elicitation: Derived business requirements from recordings of Joint Application Requirements (JAR) sessions, Joint Application Design (JAD) sessions, and meeting minutes.
- Process Modeling & Improvement: Designed as-is and to-be process flow diagrams to identify inefficiencies and drive future-state improvements.
- Analysis: Conducted fit-gap and stakeholder analyses to align solution capabilities with organizational needs.
- Requirements Translation: Converted business requirements into actionable functional, non-functional, and technical requirements.
- Stakeholder Management: Coordinated across the BA team to align priorities, manage deliverables, and maintain consistent documentation standards.
- Documentation: Produced comprehensive project artifacts to support traceability and stakeholder alignment.
